EGON SCHIELE

(June 12, 1890 – October 31, 1918)
(pronounced [ˈʃiːlə], approximately SHEE-luh)
was an Austrian painter, a protégé of Gustav Klimt,
and a major figurative painter of the early 20th century.

His work is noted for its intensity and its raw sexuality, and the many self-portraits the artist produced, including naked self-portraits. The twisted body shapes and the expressive line that characterize Schiele's paintings and drawings mark the artist as an early exponent of Expressionism. Although his first work is very much influenced by Klmint and Kokoschka, Schiele's work grew more complex and thematic, and after his imprisonment in 1912 he dealt with themes such as death and rebirth,[10] although female nudes remained his main output.
